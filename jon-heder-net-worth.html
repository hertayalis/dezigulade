    What is Jon Heder’s net worth?

    Jon Heder, the American actor and filmmaker, has a net worth of $10 million dollars. He is most famous for his role as Napoleon Dynamite in the 2004 comedy film of the same name.

    Heder has also appeared in other comedic movies such as “Just Like Heaven,” “The Benchwarmers,” “Blades of Glory,” and “School for Scoundrels.” Furthermore, he has lent his voice to animated children’s films such as “Surf’s Up” and “Monster House,” as well as TV shows like “Pickle and Peanut.”

    Jon Heder Earned Only $1,000 for “Napoleon Dynamite” Role

    Jon Heder’s breakout role in “Napoleon Dynamite” may have earned him fame, but it didn’t earn him much money. The actor was famously paid only $1,000 for his work on the independent film.

    However, despite the low pay, the movie went on to become a surprise hit, earning $44.5 million at the box office and launching Heder’s career.

    While it’s unclear how much Heder ultimately made from the film, it’s safe to say that the $1,000 he was originally paid ended up looking like quite a bargain.

    Early Life and Education

    Jon Heder was born in 1977 in Fort Collins, Colorado, and grew up in Salem, Oregon. He has five siblings, including an identical twin brother named Dan. Heder attended South Salem High School, where he was a member of the drama club and swim team, and achieved the rank of Eagle Scout. After graduating, he attended Brigham Young University and graduated in 2002. It was during his time at BYU that he befriended Jared Hess and starred in his short film “Peluca.”

    Breakout Role in “Napoleon Dynamite”

    In 2004, Jon Heder gained widespread recognition for his starring role in the independent film “Napoleon Dynamite.” The movie, which premiered at Sundance Film Festival in January of that year, features Heder as the titular character – an awkward high school student dealing with a range of personal dilemmas and quirky acquaintances.

    Despite its small budget of only $400,000, “Napoleon Dynamite” went on to become a massive financial success, grossing $46 million at the box office. Heder’s performance was widely praised, earning him a number of accolades including MTV Movie Awards for Best Breakthrough Performance and Best Musical Sequence. He also received nominations from the Teen Choice Awards.

    The film has since become a cult favorite and even spawned a short-lived animated series on Fox.

    Jon Heder’s Career Highlights

    Jon Heder’s breakout role was in the 2004 indie comedy “Napoleon Dynamite.” He later had supporting roles in “Just Like Heaven” and lent his voice to “Monster House.” In 2006, he starred in “The Benchwarmers” and “School for Scoundrels,” but both were poorly received by critics. However, he bounced back in 2007 with a leading role in the well-reviewed sports comedy “Blades of Glory” and voiced the character of Chicken Joe in “Surf’s Up.” In the early 2010s, he appeared in “When in Rome,” “For Ellen,” and provided his voice to various animated films. Later credits include “Reality,” “Walt Before Mickey,” “Christmas Eve,” “Ghost Team,” and “The Tiger Hunter.” Other movies with Heder have included “Unexpected Race” and “Killing Winston Jones.”

    Television Career

    Jon Heder made his television debut in 2004 on “Mad TV” and later appeared on “Robot Chicken” and hosted an episode of “Saturday Night Live” with musical guest Ashlee Simpson. He also made guest appearances on “My Name is Earl” and “How I Met Your Mother.” Most of Heder’s other television roles have been voiceover work for animated shows such as “The Legend of Korra,” “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles,” and “All Hail King Julien.” From 2015 to 2018, Heder voiced the main character on Disney XD’s “Pickle and Peanut.”

    Jon Heder’s Web Series Career

    Jon Heder entered the world of web series in 2008 with his horror comedy “Woke Up Dead.” He not only produced but also starred in the series. Along with his brother Dan, he also appeared in the internet martial arts comedy series “Sockbaby.” Heder has made guest appearances on various web series including “TableTop,” “Critical Role,” and hosted “Dead Grandma.”

    Personal Life

    Jon Heder met his wife Kirsten Bales while studying at Brigham Young University. They got married in 2002 and are both members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The couple has four children – two sons and two daughters.
