    Otile Brown, born Jacob Obunga on March 11, 1994, is a versatile Kenyan artist known for his soulful R&B music, captivating songwriting, and charismatic presence.

    As an independent singer, songwriter, guitarist, and actor, Otile Brown has significantly impacted the music scene in East and Central Africa.

    His melodic tunes and heartfelt lyrics have resonated with fans, solidifying his position as one of Kenya’s most promising talents.

    Early Life & Education

    Otile Brown’s current age, as of 2023, is 29. He was born on March 11, 1994, in Kisumu, Kenya; it is evident that his passion for music and performing arts has been a significant driving force throughout his life.

    He grew up in Mombasa’s Miritini neighborhood. He is the youngest of four siblings (three brothers and one sister). Otile began performing and writing music when he was 13 years old.

    Personal Life

    In terms of personal life, Otile Brown has garnered attention for his romantic relationships. He was previously in a highly publicized relationship with Kenyan socialite Vera Sidika.

    Their relationship sent the media agog, drawing controversies and speculation.

    Career

    Otile Brown’s career breakthrough came with the release of his hit single “Imaginary Love” featuring Khaligraph Jones, which catapulted him into the spotlight.

    This powerful track showcased his smooth vocals and emotional depth as a singer. Following this success, Otile released his debut studio album in April 2017, introducing fans to his unique blend of R&B melodies and heartfelt storytelling.

    Throughout his career, Otile Brown has collaborated with various artists, including Sanaipei Tande, Okangasto, Barakah The Prince, and King Kaka. These collaborations have allowed him to showcase his versatility and expand his musical horizons.

    Otile’s discography features notable songs such as “Baby Love” and “Chaguo la Moyo,” which have resonated with audiences and amassed millions of views and streams across digital platforms.

    His album “Just in Love,” released on June 3, 2020, further solidifies his position as an exceptional R&B artist in East and Central Africa.

    With each release, Otile continues to captivate listeners with his heartfelt lyrics, melodic harmonies, and soulful performances.
