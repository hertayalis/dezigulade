    John Bannister Goodenough, shortly John Goodenough, was an American scientist. He specialized in materials and solid-state physics.

    Goodenough was a Nobel laureate in chemistry. The Nobel Prize receiver is widely acknowledged for his pivotal role in identifying and advancing lithium-ion battery technology.

    Goodenough held Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, and Materials Science professorships at the University of Texas in Austin.

    Moreover, the man is recognized for developing the Goodenough–Kanamori rules. His groundbreaking contribution to making computer random-access memory (RAM) pushed the century’s technology ahead.

    He graduated from Yale University. Eventually, he served as a meteorologist in the US military during World War II and continued his service further.

    Also, The John B. Goodenough Award in materials science is named after him for his contributions.

    In 2019, John was honored with the Nobel Prize in Chemistry, making him the oldest Nobel laureate in history at 97.

    From August 27, 2021, until his passing, he was the oldest living Nobel Prize laureate.

    In addition, Goodenough received prestigious honors, including the National Medal of Science, the Fermi Award, the Draper Prize, the Japan Prize, and the Copley Medal.
